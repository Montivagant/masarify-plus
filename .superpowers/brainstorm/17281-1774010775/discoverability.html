A

"Smart Dashboard Cards" — Surface Insights on Home

Add contextual cards to the dashboard that appear when relevant:

  • Budget Alert Card: "You've spent 85% of your Food budget" — appears when spending > 80% of any budget
  • Goal Progress Card: "Laptop Fund: 65% saved (2,340 EGP to go)" — shows active goal with highest progress
  • Recurring Due Card: "Netflix due tomorrow (149 EGP)" — shows next upcoming bill
  • AI Insight Card: "You spend 30% more on weekends" — rotating insight from background AI
  • Streak Card: "5-day logging streak! Keep it up" — engagement mechanic

Cards appear/disappear based on data. No data = no card. Max 2-3 cards at a time.

Pros

  • Features come to the user (no navigation needed)
  • Cards are actionable (tap to see more)
  • Progressive — more data = richer dashboard
  • AI insights already computed (background_ai_provider)

Cons

  • Dashboard could feel cluttered with too many cards
  • Need careful priority/ordering logic
B

"Contextual Nudges" — Suggest Features at the Right Moment

Instead of cards on the dashboard, show subtle nudges at specific trigger points:

  • After 5th expense in same category: "Want to set a budget for Food?" (bottom banner)
  • After first income transaction: "Start a savings goal?" (inline suggestion)
  • After 3rd manual entry today: "Try voice input — just say it" (toast with mic icon)
  • After first SMS detected: "We found bank transactions in your SMS" (notification card)
  • On bill due date: Local notification "Netflix due today (149 EGP)"

Nudges are dismissable, one-time per trigger, and stored in preferences.

Pros

  • Feels organic — right feature at right time
  • Doesn't clutter dashboard
  • Educational without being annoying

Cons

  • Users might dismiss before reading
  • Complex trigger logic to implement
  • Features not visible until triggered
C

"Both" — Dashboard Cards + Contextual Nudges

Combine both approaches with clear rules:

  • Dashboard: Max 2 smart cards (budget alert + AI insight OR goal progress). Rotate daily.
  • Nudges: Feature discovery nudges at trigger points (voice, SMS, budgets, goals). One-time, dismissable.
  • Rule: Never show a card AND a nudge for the same feature simultaneously

Dashboard cards = ongoing value. Nudges = one-time feature education.

Pros

  • Best of both — ongoing relevance + discovery moments
  • Dashboard stays useful long-term
  • Nudges handle first-time education

Cons

  • Most implementation effort
  • Need careful UX to avoid notification fatigue

Recommendation: Option A (Smart Dashboard Cards)

Dashboard cards are the highest-impact, most sustainable approach. You already have the AI providers computing insights — they just need a UI surface. Nudges (Option B) can be added later as a Phase 2 enhancement. Start with 3-4 card types, max 2 visible at once, priority-ordered.
